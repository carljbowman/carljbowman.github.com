Oatmeal Muffins

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Oatmeal
  • 1 cup Sour Milk
  • 1 Egg
  • ½ cup Brown Sugar
  • ½ cup Vegetable Oil
  • 1 cup Sifted Flour
  • 2 tsp Baking Powder
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • ½ tsp Cinnamon

Prepwork

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
  2. Soak oats in sour milk for 1 hour; add eggs and beat well. Add sugar and mix. Add vegetable oil
  3. Add flour sifted with baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
  4. Fill greased muffin pans ⅔ full.
  5. Bake in hot oven for 15 to 20 minutes.

Banana Nut Bread

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup Vegetable
  • ¾ cup Sugar
  • 1 cup Bran (Any Type)
  • 2 tbs Water
  • 1 ½ cups Mashed Bananas
  • 1 ½ cups Sifted Flour
  • 2 tsp Baking Soda
  • ¼ tsp Salt
  • ½ tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 tsp Vinilla
  • ½ cup Chopped Nuts

Prepwork

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Cream oil and sugar; add egg, then bran; mix throughly.
  3. Combine water and bananas; add alternately with sifted dry ingredients. Mix throughly; add vinilla and nuts.
  4. Bake in greased 9 ½" x 5 ¼" x 2 ¾" loaf pan in moderate oven for 1 hour.

Polpette Alla Casalinga (Meatballs)

Ingredients

  • 2 slices French or Italian bread torn into small pieces
  • 1 Egg lightly beaten
  • 1 lbs Ground Beef chuck ground twice
  • ¼ lb Sweet Italian Sausage
  • 6 tbs freshly grated Parmesan Cheese
  • 2 tbs finely chopped Garlic
  • 1 tbs Olive Oil
  • 1 t grated Lemon Peel
  • ¼ ground Allspice
  • 1 t Salt
  • Freshly Ground Pepper
  • ½ cup Milk
  • 2 tbs finely chopped Parsley

Prepwork

  1. Soak pieces of bread in ½ cup Milk for 5 minutes, then squeeze them dry and discard the milk. In a large mixing bowl, combine the soaked bread, the beaten eggs, 1lb of ground beef ¼ lb of italian sausage, garlic, lemon peel, allspice, salt, and a few grinds of black peper. Knead the mixture vigorously with both hands or beat with a wooden spoon until all of the ingredients are well blended and the mixture is smooth and fluffy.
  2. Shape the mixture into small balls about 1½ inches in diameter. Lay the meatballs out in one layer on a flay tray or baking sheet, cover them with plastic wrap and chill for at least one hour.
  3. Head ¼ c of olive oil or vegetable oil in a heavy 10 to 12 inch skillet until a light haze forms over it. Fry the meat balls 5 or 6 at a time over moderately high heat, shaking the pan constantly to roll the balls and help keep them round. In 8 to 10 minuetes the meatballs should be brown outside and show no trace of pink inside. Add more olive or vegetable oil to the skillet as needed.
