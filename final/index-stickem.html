Congressional Productivity

2013

Using the Resume of Congressional Activity as a database this project looked at developing an interface to interact with this data in a visual manner.

The interface to the left looks to examine the story of Congress from 1947 to the present. This curated experience of the information analyzes the relationship between the number of measures enacted vs introduced as the basis for annual comparison.

The navigation in exploring this information is built into a graph comparing the number of hours spent in session with the number of measures enacted within both the House of Representatives and the Senate.

This driving force gives insight into the relationship of the checks and balances between opposing sections of Congress; while also setting up the user with a foundation of information to dissect into a story.

The visual tool above looks at an annual breakdown of the number of measures introduced versus enacted. One can use draw connections between these relationships to other contextual events and information exposing trends.

Like the scale, this tool visualizes the political majority within the House and the Senate. These tools can be viewed as static images but when the user slides the date slider they can watch these elements shift relating to the timeline.

This video explores the interface further exposing more features and ways to interact with the topic.

External Oil Price

2013

This project demanded a typographic timeline installation viewing ten past events. This subversive installation exposes very relevant information about oil catastrophes. This installation looks to create a experience for the user to compare their individual oil consumption and cost to a the larger than life external costs taking a toll on human and animal lives and our planet.

The secondary piece to the installation is a book that examines both the topic as a whole but also the process is developing the installation. While demonstrating this information the book embodies similar relieves and exposure of seemingly forgotten costs of an addiction.

Internal spreads act as an opportunity to the reader to re-expose themselves to images of past but still damaging oil catastrophies.

Both the installation and book pose a question about the media cycle looking to examine relationship between insurmountable numbers and their relationship to the individual. No longer do we hear about the BP Oil spill even after two years; a spill that bled a total of 39 million gallons of oil. Now compare that with the 46 year old spill of Torrey Canyon which leaked 38 million gallons of oil. A spill 1 million gallons smaller and a half decade old still plagues it's environment leaving entire rock quarries full of oil.

Sustain(ability)

2012

Inspired by the works and writing of Jared Diamond, this project sought to look at the nine past societies and their detrimental habits, addictions, and flaws leading to their inevitable collapse.

The first half of the project is a book dissecting key societal issues withing the overall system of downfall. Then relates those flaws to relevant current global problems we are facing today.

After developing the book as a traditional printed medium with written content, the next step was to tell this story on a time based digital platform.

This animation looks at the individual downfall of Easter Island, specifically the their trends within the system of collapsing societies.

Chairs by Eames

2012

We were asked to pick a 2D print design and narrate an animated story in dialog with the piece.

As a designer it is hard not to be inspired by the prolific works of Ray and Charles Eames. This project is an homage to their remarkable chair designs.

Alluding to other works by Eames such as the Power of Ten, this piece explores their process in a scaled manner The choice to score the piece with a modern up beat tempo relating to their timeless quality and their works relevance both in art and design.

Post Era

2012

The parameters of this project asked for a data set of roughly 100 objects of which had seven attributes; resulting in 700 pieces of data relating to the original 100.

This section of the project plotted the distance traveled in comparison with the bearing of each individual postcard. The length of the line represents the distance travels and the angle of the line is the bearing.

All the points plotted then are color coated to the decade they were sent. Their is a gradient from light to dark within those years lighter early years to darker later years within that specific decade.

Every point in which a post card ends it's journey a gray ring marks the distance across the entire diagram. These lines build up to show average distances of these postcards regardless of their bearing.

Each card deviates from the a central point as if all points of origin were set equal. The angle of the line translates into the bearing in which the postcard traveled. Around the edges of the main diagram every bearing degree is marked.

The final element of this project was to develop a iPhone app that would serve as a searchable data base.

My approach was to create an intuitive visual easy to use system of filtering for both new and advanced users. And not allow finger placement to affect the overall usability and display of nessasary information.
